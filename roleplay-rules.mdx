_These rules govern roleplay interactions, ensuring scenarios are conducted fairly, realistically, and in a manner that promotes immersive and enjoyable roleplay for everyone._

### **1. Warning Fire Rule**

A Warning Fire is a final warning given before initiating lethal force. It must provide the opposing player with a reasonable opportunity to surrender, comply, or react.

- Warning fire must be fired **in the direction of the player**, without intentionally hitting or injuring them.
- Warning shots may be fired **into the ground or the air**, provided they are clearly visible and audible to the opposing player.
- A minimum requirement for fired as a warning.
  - **Exception:** If using a pistol, a minimum of **3 Bullet** is required.
  - **For other Weapon:** **10 Bullet** is required.
- If the opposing player **draws a weapon, aims a weapon, or attempts to fire**, you may immediately engage without completing the remaining warning shots.

**Ongoing Situations:**

Warning fire is **not required** if both parties are already involved in the **same active roleplay scenario** where hostile intent has already been established.

### **2. POV Rule (Point of View)**

Players must provide a **valid POV (video recording)** whenever a roleplay dispute or rule violation is reported. A POV is the primary evidence used by **Shadow** or the **FRP Team** to determine what occurred.

- A **valid POV is mandatory** for any report involving **Fail RP** or any other rule violation.
- The recording must clearly show the **entire incident**, including the events **before, during, and after** the situation.
- The POV must be a **full-screen recording** with **clear game audio**.
- Recordings that are cropped, edited, muted, or manipulated may be rejected.
- If requested by **Shadow** or the **FRP Team**, the POV must be submitted within the specified time.
- **Both parties** involved in the report may be required to submit their POV.
- Failure to provide a valid POV when requested may result in a **Normal Ban / PvP Ban(Decided By FRP Team)**.

### **3. Shadows Spot Action**

A **Shadow Action** is an immediate punishment issued by Shadows for specific rule violations.

- **No warning will be given** for:
  - Vulgar or offensive language.
  - Parental abuse.
- Players may **not request, demand, or pressure Admins** to take immediate action during ongoing RP or PvP scenarios.

Players who repeatedly commit FRP after receiving a warning may receive a **Shadow Action** without further notice.

### **4. Dead Carry Rule**

Dead Carry is the act of carrying an unconscious or dead player. It must only be used for realistic roleplay purposes and not to exploit game mechanics.

- **Using a vehicle to dead carry a player is considered Bug Exploitation and is strictly prohibited.**
- **Dead Carry Hostage Roleplay is not allowed.** Unconscious or dead players cannot be taken or treated as hostages or used for negotiations.
- Dead players may only be carried for legitimate roleplay purposes, such as moving them to a safe location or for EMS/police procedures.
- Players may not abuse the dead carry mechanic to escape, transport, or gain an unfair advantage during an active scenario.

### **5. Admin Involvement Policy**

- Admin assistance may only be requested if you are **unable to continue playing due to a bug, glitch, or other technical issue**.
- **Do not call for Admins during any gang situation.**
- If you are unsure about a rule, **refer to the server rulebook** instead of interrupting Admins during an active situation.
- During gang situations, **Admins will not intervene in Fail Roleplay incidents**. Continue the situation and submit an **FRP ticket** after it has concluded.
- If a situation becomes **uncontrollable, severely disruptive, or threatens server stability**, Admins reserve the **full authority to intervene and make a final decision**. Their decision is final and must be respected.

### 6. Citizen vs. Citizen Interaction

#### Participation Limits

- A maximum of **4 participants** may be involved in a single Citizen vs. Citizen situation.
- A maximum of **2 ground vehicles** may be used.
- Citizens may **not** participate in more than **one active situation** at the same time.

#### Asset & Role Restrictions

- The use of **gang assets, gang attire, or gang communication channels** is strictly prohibited.
- Citizen situations must remain **civilian-level Roleplay**.
- Militarized behavior, organized combat tactics, or high-lethality actions are not permitted.

#### Hostility & Escalation

- Citizen vs. Citizen interactions are **non-hostile by default**.
- Any escalation must be supported by **proper Roleplay development** and a **valid in-character (IC) reason**.
- Immediate aggression or violence without sufficient RP justification is considered **Fail Roleplay (FRP)**.

#### Enforcement & Abuse Prevention

The following actions are strictly prohibited:

- Using alternate characters to bypass participation limits.
- Using middlemen or proxy players to circumvent the rules.
- Using out-of-character (OOC) communication to gain an unfair advantage.

Any attempt to bypass these rules or disrupt server realism, balance, or Roleplay quality will be treated as **Major Fail Roleplay (Major FRP)**.

### Note

- A Citizen vs. Citizen situation **may not exceed 4 participants** under any circumstances.
- Exceeding the participant or vehicle limits will render the situation **invalid** and may result in administrative action.
