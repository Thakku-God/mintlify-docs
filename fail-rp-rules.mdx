_No two FRPs are the same. Situations and their contexts differ; therefore, quoting previous FRPs or actions for personal benefit will not be considered._

## 1. Time Limits & Ticket Creation

### FRP Ticket Rules

1. FRP tickets must be raised within **4 hours** of the incident — no exceptions.
2. The **4-hour** window is strictly for reporting and clearly describing the event.
3. POVs must be submitted within **12 hours** of ticket creation.
4. Actions will be taken for all the FRPs that are shown/visible in the POVs submitted.

**Example:**

If the situation occurred at **1:00 PM**, you have until **5:00 PM** (same day) to raise the ticket. If you created the ticket at **1:00 PM**, you will have until **1:00 AM** (12 hours from ticket creation) to submit your POVs.

Once an FRP is called, you may not continue the same or any related situation until the case is fully resolved and actions are implemented by the F-TEAM.

FRP discussion slots are fixed from **6:00 PM to 8:00 PM** daily, unless announced otherwise by the F-TEAM.

## 2. POV (Proof of Violation / Point of View)

POVs must be submitted within **12 hours** of ticket generation.

### Accepted Ways

1. Video.
2. YouTube (Unlisted/Public).
3. Google Drive (Public access required).
4. OneDrive.
5. Any permanent share link.

### Not Accepted

WeTransfer, MediaFire, or any expirable links — Ticket will be auto-closed.

### Audio Required

The POV must include the voice of the person recording, even if not directly involved.

Missing or unclear audio = Invalid POV.

### Content Rules

1. The POV must show the entire situation — before, during, and after the violation. Unedited and uncut clips only.
2. If the provided Google Drive link is not accessible to everyone, the POV will not be reviewed, and the F-TEAM will not request access.
3. Additional POVs submitted after **12 hours** will not be considered.
4. POVs from other player's or admin's streams are not valid, unless approved by Management in exceptional cases.

## 3. Details to Include in Your Report

When raising a ticket:

1. **Other Violations:** Clearly explain what happened and how it breaks RP rules.
2. **Time of Incident:** Specify the exact date and time (in IST) of the incident.
3. **Vulgar Language:** Mention the exact words used.
4. **Issue Type:** Mention the FRP(s) raised while creating the ticket. Any additional violations added later in the chat may not be considered.
5. **Report Detail:** Not describing the events or situation properly will cause delays in handling your ticket. Be detailed so the FRP Responder can clearly understand your side.
6. **Timestamp(s) of the FRP(s):** Exact timestamp(s) of each FRP, including the situation starting time, must be mentioned.

**Format:** `FRP Type : HH:MM` _(Example: NO FEAR RP : 12:15)_

## 4. Absence, Closure & Complaints

1. Voluntary absence for **24 hours** after being called for FRP discussion will lead to automatic action implementation based on available evidence.
2. Once this happens, there will be no discussion regarding the FRP for which the ban or action was implemented.
3. If a person is called to the FRP Voice Channel and fails to appear, it will result in temporary Whitelist revocation until the case is resolved.
4. Unnecessary or spam appeals will result in additional bans or penalties.
5. If the ticket raiser provides no response or fails to submit the requested POV, the ticket will be closed and cannot be reopened.

## 5. FRP Actions

1. FRP actions taken by the F-TEAM will be final.
2. All actions will be taken by examining the POVs provided in the FRP ticket.
3. No appeals are available after the action is taken by the FRP Responder.
4. If you feel the F-TEAM's decision is biased, you may approach Management for review.
