_These rules are the foundation of roleplay on the server. Every player is expected to understand and follow them at all times. Failure to comply may result in warnings, Shadow Actions, or other punish_

## **1. Random Deathmatch (RDM)**

Random Deathmatch (RDM) is attacking, injuring, or killing another player without a valid in-character (IC) roleplay reason.

Players must always have a realistic roleplay reason before initiating violence.

#### **Examples of RDM**

- Shooting someone because they looked at you.
- Punching or stabbing random players.
- Killing someone without any interaction or roleplay.
- Shooting someone simply because they insulted you in OOC.

#### **Valid RP Example**

- A robbery turns violent after negotiations fail.
- Gang rivalry resulting from ongoing roleplay.
- Self-defense during an active RP scenario.

## **2. Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM)**

Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM) is intentionally using a vehicle as a weapon to injure or kill another player without a valid roleplay reason.

Vehicles should primarily be used for transportation.

#### **Examples**

- Running over players intentionally.
- Driving into crowds to kill players.
- Using a vehicle to avoid gunfights.

#### **Allowed**

Accidents during normal driving are not considered VDM if they were genuinely unintentional.

## **3. Power Gaming**

Power Gaming is the act of forcing unrealistic actions or outcomes onto another player, or abusing game mechanics, server features, or a lack of communication to gain an unfair advantage during roleplay.

Roleplay should always provide all involved players with a fair opportunity to react and respond.

## **4. Metagaming (Meta)**

Metagaming is using information your character would not realistically know.

Only use information your character has learned through in-character roleplay.

#### **Examples**

- Using Discord voice chat to locate players.
- Watching a stream to find someone's location.
- Using OOC chat to gain IC information.
- Recognizing masked players by their voice or username.

## **5. Fear Roleplay (FearRP)**

FearRP means your character must realistically fear death, serious injury, or dangerous situations.

#### **Examples**

You should:

- Put your hands up when heavily outnumbered.
- Cooperate if escape is impossible.
- Act injured when seriously wounded.

#### **Invalid Examples**

- Laughing at someone pointing a gun at your head.
- Starting a fight while surrounded by armed players.
- Ignoring threats without any realistic reason.

## **6. Ruleplaying**

Ruleplaying is abusing or twisting server rules to gain an unfair advantage instead of creating realistic roleplay.

#### **Examples**

- Arguing technicalities to avoid RP.
- Exploiting loopholes in the rules.
- Using rules against their intended purpose.

Always follow both the **letter** and **spirit** of the rules.

## **7. Combat Logging**

Players are **not allowed to leave the server** while engaged in an active roleplay scenario.

- If your game crashes or you disconnect due to network issues, you **must rejoin the server within 15 minutes** and return to your last location to continue the roleplay.
- If you are unable to rejoin within 15 minutes, you must **notify the staff in the appropriate voice channel or support channel within 5 minutes** of disconnecting.
- Failing to return or report your disconnect may be treated as **combat logging** and may result in FRP action.

## **8. No Toxic Roleplay**

Roleplay conflict is acceptable.

Personal attacks are not.

Players may not:

- Use parental abuse.
- Use hate speech.
- Harass players through voice or text.
- Be excessively toxic.
- Mock, insult, or make fun of another player's disability, medical condition, or personal circumstances.
- Make sexually explicit, degrading, or inappropriate comments toward female characters or any player.

**Vulgar language and parental abuse are subject to immediate Shadow Action without warning.**

## **9. Common Sense**

Not every possible situation can be covered by written rules.

Players are expected to use common sense and prioritize realistic, enjoyable, and fair roleplay.

If an action would be unreasonable in real life, it is likely not appropriate in roleplay.

## **10. Lore Breaking**

Lore Breaking is interfering with or disrupting another player's or faction's ongoing roleplay without a valid in-character reason, causing the roleplay story or immersion to be broken.

#### **Examples**

- Interrupting a proposal, wedding, funeral, or family RP to start your own scenario.
- Interfering in an active RP that does not involve your character.
- Calling Police or EMS repeatedly with fake emergencies to disrupt their roleplay.
- Forcing Police, EMS, or Government factions to break their SOP (Standard Operating Procedures) or roleplay restrictions without a valid RP reason.
- Ruining another player's ongoing storyline or character development by intentionally disrupting their scene.

## **11. Impure Roleplay**

Impure Roleplay is creating or participating in roleplay that negatively affects, exploits, or breaks the quality and immersion of an ongoing roleplay scenario.

#### **Examples**

- Using emotes or F10 Menu, player IDs, or name tags to identify a player your character does not know.
- Using Out-of-Character (OOC) information, Discord, or external communication to gain an advantage.
- Abusing game mechanics, player collisions, animations, or character model bugs during roleplay.
- Creating non-negotiable roleplay scenarios that force only one outcome and give no opportunity for the other party to respond.
- Exploiting server features or mechanics instead of resolving situations through realistic roleplay.

## **12. New Life Rule**

The New Life Rule (NLR) means that when your character dies and is revived or respawns, they lose all memory of the events leading up to their death. You must not return to continue or seek revenge in the same roleplay scenario.

- You lose all memory of the events that led to your death.
- You may not return to the location of your death or any continuation of the same scenario.
- You may not seek revenge or participate in the same incident after being revived or respawning.
- **Even if another player witnessed your death, you may not use their information to continue the previous scenario.**
- Witnesses may continue the roleplay based on what they witnessed, **but the deceased player cannot rejoin or influence that scenario in any way.**
- If your character is  burned during a roleplay situation, you must respawn. Upon respawning, the New Life Rule will immediately take effect, and you must follow the New Life Rule

## **13. Bot Revive**

A **Bot Revive** is a revival performed through the server's revive system instead of being revived by EMS. Players who choose a Bot Revive are considered to have received medical treatment off-screen and must follow the server's roleplay rules.

- Using a **Bot Revive** ends your current roleplay scenario.
- After a Bot Revive, you must **not return** to the location of your death or continue the same situation.
- You must follow the **New Life Rule (NLR)** after receiving a Bot Revive.
- You may not use information from the previous scenario after being revived.
- Do not use a Bot Revive to avoid EMS roleplay or active roleplay situations.

### **13.1 Admin Revive**

- If a player is revived by an admin due to any bug, script issue, or failure of the automatic revive system at the time of any active situation, the revive will be treated exactly the same as a bot revive.
- The player must continue roleplay under the same conditions, rights, and limitations of the bot revive. An admin revive is solely intended to resolve technical issues and does not provide any additional roleplay advantage or alter the situation's outcome.

## **14. Safe Zone Rules**

Safe Zones are designated areas where hostile or criminal roleplay is prohibited.

- No shooting, fighting, robbing, kidnapping, or any hostile roleplay inside a Safe Zone.
- Players may not use a Safe Zone to escape or avoid an active roleplay scenario.
- If a roleplay situation begins inside a Safe Zone, all involved players **must move at least 100 meters away** before continuing the scenario.
- **No firing, taking hostages, surrounding players, aiming weapons, or initiating any criminal activity within 100 meters (360**° **Direction) of a Safe Zone.**
- Camping, baiting, or waiting outside a Safe Zone to immediately attack players leaving the area is prohibited.
- Players inside a Safe Zone may not provoke others to start a hostile roleplay.
- If a roleplay situation is **triggered inside a Safe Zone**, all involved players must immediately move the scenario outside the Safe Zone before continuing. Hostile actions such as fighting, shooting, kidnapping, or robbing must not take place inside the Safe Zone.

#### **Examples**

**Valid**

- Two players have an argument inside a Safe Zone and move **100 meters away** before continuing the confrontation.

Safe Zone Area:

- All Garages
- Impounds
- Police Stations
- City Hall
- Jail
- All Whitelisted Job Area's
- Hospitals
- Business Working Space's
- Event areas
- All Shop Interiors (Dress Shop , Tatoo Shop , Barber Shop)

## **15. Situation Continuation Rule (Rule of 24)**

Any war, gang conflict, or ongoing hostile roleplay scenario may continue for a maximum of **24 hours** from the time it begins.

After the 24-hour period has elapsed:

- The conflict is considered **concluded**, and all parties must immediately cease hostilities.
- Previous events may **not** be used to justify violence, kidnapping, robbery, retaliation, or any other hostile actions.
- Any further interaction must begin as a **new roleplay scenario** with fresh initiation, following all applicable server rules.
- Players may not claim that an old conflict is still active to bypass server rules, including initiation or warning-fire requirements.
- Any individual, group, or gang found intentionally extending or reviving a conflict beyond the 24-hour limit may be subject to **Fail Roleplay** punishment.

Exception: This rule does not apply to normal day-to-day roleplay interactions or hostage situations, which may continue naturally as dictated by the roleplay scenario and server rules.

## **16. Stream Sniping**

Stream Sniping is the act of watching or using another player's livestream, broadcast, or shared screen to gain information or an unfair advantage during roleplay.

- Players may not use information obtained from a livestream or screen share to influence in-character actions.
- Do not locate, track, or identify players by watching their stream.
- Do not use stream chat, Discord screen sharing, or any live broadcast to gain tactical information.
- If you are involved in a roleplay scenario with a streamer, you must rely only on information your character has obtained through legitimate roleplay.
- Intentionally delaying your stream to avoid detection does **not** permit others to use stream information.

## **16. Out of Character (OOC)**

Out of Character refers to the act of you the player playing as yourself, not your character. OOC occurs when:

- You mention events, places or occurrences that have not happened in the city that your character has not experienced.
- You mention a player’s or group’s real life circumstances
- You mention out of city words, some examples include: FRP, FRP names, etc.
- Using any Information from your alternate characters into other characters. (Both Characters are unknown to each other).

The basis of Roleplay is that you are roleplaying as a character, you yourself are not the character. So understand the difference before continuing your roleplay.

## **17. AFK (Away From Keyboard) Rule**

AFK (Away From Keyboard) means leaving your character unattended while remaining connected to the server.

- Players should **not remain AFK for extended periods** while in-game. You may be automatically kicked by the server.
- If you must go AFK, move your character to a **safe, low-traffic location** away from other players and active roleplay.
- **Do not go AFK during an active roleplay scenario**, including combat, police interactions, robberies, or negotiations.
- Players are responsible for the safety of their character while AFK.
- If your character is robbed, kidnapped, injured, or otherwise affected while you are AFK, **you are responsible for the consequences**. Staff will not refund items or reverse roleplay because you were AFK.

## **18. Respawning Rule**

Respawning is the act of using the server's respawn or Bot Revive system after your character has been incapacitated or declared dead.

- Players must respawn only when permitted by server rules or when instructed by **Shadow**.
- **Respawning is mandatory** if your character is **burned beyond recovery**, trapped in an unrecoverable situation, or any other scenario where the server rules require a respawn.
- Players may not refuse a mandatory respawn to continue an unrealistic roleplay scenario.
- By respawning or using a **Bot Revive**, you agree to follow the **New Life Rule**.
- Respawning immediately ends your involvement in the current roleplay scenario.
- You may not respawn to avoid arrest, kidnapping, robbery, or any active roleplay unless permitted by server rules or instructed by shadow.

Exception: This rule does not apply to normal day-to-day roleplay interactions or hostage situations, which may continue naturally as dictated by the roleplay scenario and server rules.

## **19. Cop Baiting**

Cop Baiting is the act of intentionally provoking, harassing, or luring Police into unnecessary roleplay or pursuits without a valid in-character reason.

- Players may not intentionally provoke Police to start a chase, shootout, or arrest without a legitimate RP reason.
- Repeatedly insulting, taunting, or disrupting Police solely to gain attention or create unnecessary RP is prohibited.
- Players may not commit minor crimes repeatedly for the sole purpose of attracting Police.
- Using Police as entertainment or wasting their time with false or meaningless interactions is considered Cop Baiting.
- Any interaction with Police must have a genuine roleplay purpose.

## **20. Hostage Rules**

A hostage is a player taken against their will to support a legitimate criminal roleplay scenario. Hostage situations must be realistic, fair, and provide opportunities for negotiation.

- A hostage may only be taken for a **valid In-Character reason**.
- Players must value the hostage's life and avoid unnecessary harm.
- Hostages may not be executed without a valid RP reason or after negotiations have completely failed.
- Dead, unconscious, newly revived, or AFK players **cannot** be taken hostage.
- Hostages must be treated realistically and given a fair opportunity to roleplay.
- Police and criminals must negotiate in good faith before using lethal force whenever possible.
- A hostage may not be used repeatedly to farm demands or unnecessarily prolong a scenario.
- Players may not create non-negotiable hostage scenarios that only favor one side.
- Taking hostages inside **Safe Zones** or within the **100-meter (360**° **Direction) Safe Zone buffer** is prohibited.
- Hostage roleplay must comply with all server rules, including **Power Gaming** , **FearRP**, and **Value of Life**.
- **Dead Carry Hostage is strictly prohibited.**
- A hostage must remain involved in the scenario until they are released, rescued, or the situation has concluded.
- Hostage situations must have a legitimate criminal objective and may not be created solely to bait Police or prolong roleplay.
- **Cross Hostage is strictly prohibited.** Players may not exchange hostages or take a hostage solely in response to another hostage situation to gain an advantage.
- **Double Hostage is not allowed.** Only one hostage is permitted per scenario unless explicitly approved by Shadow or during an official server event.
- A hostage may be held for a **maximum of 45 minutes**. Once the 45-minute limit is reached, the scenario **must be concluded** through negotiation, rescue, surrender, or release.

**Exception:** If the hostage fails to exhibit **FearRP** or intentionally disrupts the roleplay by acting unrealistically, the hostage duration may be extended **only under the supervision and approval of an administrator**. The extension will be determined based on the circumstances of the situation.

### **Hostage Demands**

- Criminals may demand a maximum of **₹15,00,000 (15 Lakh)** worth of cash **(white money only in the case of MCPD)**, items, or assets.
- Hostage demands may only be communicated through:
  - Phone calls.
  - Direct in-character (voice) communication.
- Using text chat to make hostage demands is prohibited.
- Hostage demands must remain realistic, directly related to the ongoing roleplay scenario, and within the scope of what law enforcement can reasonably negotiate.

### **Hostage Demand Restrictions**

During any hostage or robbery scenario, players may **not** make demands that directly interfere with the normal operations, staffing, or command structure of the MCPD.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- Demanding that specific MCPD officers respond or leave the scene.
- Players cannot take a hostage to demand the release of a player from jail or request immunity from law enforcement.
- Demanding the promotion, demotion, suspension, or dismissal of any MCPD officer.
- Demanding changes to MCPD policies, procedures, or Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs).
- Requiring the appointment or removal of command staff.
- Demanding confidential police information, internal communications, or database access.
- Demanding unrealistic or out-of-character actions that would not reasonably be accepted by law enforcement.
- MCPD-issued equipment and confiscated items cannot be demanded, requested, or negotiated for during any roleplay situation, including hostage negotiations.

### **Hostage Treatment**

- Hostages must be treated realistically throughout the scenario.
- Executing a hostage without a valid roleplay reason is prohibited.
- Hostages should be given a fair opportunity to comply with demands.

### **Negotiations**

- Robbers must allow reasonable time for negotiations.
- Law enforcement must attempt negotiation before using force whenever possible.

### **Abuse of Hostages**

- Taking hostages solely to avoid arrest, escape minor traffic stops, or grief other players is prohibited.

### **Conflict Duration**

- Gang wars and ongoing hostile conflicts may continue for a maximum of **24 hours** from the time they begin.
- After 24 hours, the conflict is considered concluded. Any continuation requires a new valid roleplay reason.
- This rule does not apply to normal day-to-day roleplay or standard hostage negotiations.

### **Staff Authority**

- Staff members have the final decision in disputes involving roleplay scenarios and hostage situations.
- Failure to follow staff instructions may result in disciplinary action.

### **Combat Logging During a Hostage Situation**

Any player who intentionally disconnects (Combat Logs) during an active hostage scenario will receive severe punishment.

#### **Punishment**

- Immediate disciplinary action, which may include a **normal ban or Indefinite Gang Restriction**, if applicable.

The hostage scenario **will continue** using a replacement hostage. The situation **will not be cancelled** because a player disconnected.

### **Hostage Roleplay Boundaries**

The following actions are **strictly prohibited** during hostage roleplay under all circumstances:

- Any form of humiliation roleplay.
- Forcing a hostage to lick shoes or perform degrading acts.
- Urinating, defecating, or simulating any similar degrading act on a hostage.
- Forcing a hostage to post specific messages in in-game chat.
- Any form of sexual, discriminatory, or excessively abusive roleplay.
- Torture roleplay that exceeds reasonable server standards or forces extreme humiliation.

### **Protected Players**

- Players are **strictly prohibited** from taking the following individuals as hostages under any circumstances:
  - Government Officials
  - Judges
  - MCMS Personnel
  - MCPD Personnel
  - Any other government-authorized employees
  - Gang Leaders
  - Gang Co-Leaders Players may not target protected individuals to bypass gang restrictions or gain an unfair advantage during negotiations. **Hostage Replacement:** Hostage replacement is permitted; however, players may **not** request, demand, or select a specific individual to act as the replacement hostage.

> **Note:** Hostage roleplay is intended to create meaningful and realistic negotiations. Any abuse of hostage mechanics, unreasonable demands, or attempts to exploit these rules may result in **Shadow Action**, **Gang Restrictions**, or additional disciplinary action depending on the severity of the violation.

## **21. Robbery Rules**

### **Breaking Robbery Rules**

To maintain fairness between **Police** and **Robbers**, the following restrictions apply during any robbery.

### **Prohibited During a Robbery**

The following vehicles and assets **may not be used** during a robbery unless explicitly approved for a pre-planned RP event:

- **Gang Assets**, including gang uniforms, gang vehicles, and gang communication channels.
- **Donator/Custom Vehicles** (including exclusive or premium vehicles obtained through donations).
- **Job Vehicles**, unless the robbery is a **pre-planned RP robbery** approved by **Shadows.**
- **Spawned Vehicles**, unless the robbery is a **pre-planned RP robbery** approved by **Shadows.**

> **Note:** All robbery participants must use **regular civilian vehicles** unless specific approval has been granted by **Shadows** for a pre-planned roleplay event. Using prohibited vehicles or assets during a robbery may result in **Shadow Action** or cancellation of the robbery scenario.

### **Robbery Limits**

- Players must **not exceed the maximum robbery participant limit** under any circumstances.
- Exceptions are only permitted for **pre-planned RP robberies** that have received prior approval from **Shadows.**
- If the robbery limit is exceeded without approval, **MCPD is authorized to respond with all available units**, and disciplinary action may be taken against the robbers.

> **Note:** Any robbery that violates these rules may be considered **Fail Roleplay** and may result in **Shadows Action**, cancellation of the robbery, or other disciplinary action.
