These rules govern official gang meetings, including their purpose, conduct, and restrictions, to ensure fair and realistic roleplay.

### **1. When a Meeting Is Required**

- If the **Pre-War Phase** ends with the opposing gang being **completely eliminated (all down)**, **no gang meeting is required**. The situation is considered concluded.
- If the **Pre-War Phase** does **not** end with the opposing gang being all down, a **mandatory gang meeting** must be held before the situation can continue.

#### **2. Meeting Rules**

- Once a gang meeting is called, **all hostile actions must immediately stop**.
- Both gangs must arrive at the meeting location within **15 minutes**.
- Attendance is **mandatory** for both gangs.
- During the meeting, both gangs may decide whether to proceed with the war or resolve the conflict peacefully.
- The meeting may only end in one of the following outcomes:
  - **War**
  - **Compromise**

### **3. Meeting Restrictions**

- A maximum of **5 members per gang** may attend.
- Only the **Gang Leader** or an **authorized representative** may speak during the meeting.
- The meeting location is considered a **temporary peace zone**.
- **No firing, attacking, or hostile actions** are permitted:
  - After the meeting has been called,
  - During the meeting,
  - Or immediately after the meeting concludes.
- Meetings must be conducted **in person**.
- **Phone calls are not permitted** during the meeting.
- Gangs may use **phones or gang chat only to coordinate the meeting location**.
- If a gang refuses the meeting but enters or remains within the meeting area, the meeting will be **automatically considered accepted**.

### **4. Inter-Gang Communication**

Gang chat is intended only for **coordination, diplomacy, and general communication** between gangs.

#### **Permitted Uses**

- Diplomacy and negotiations.
- Planning and coordination.
- Arranging gang meetings.
- General gang-related discussions.

#### **Prohibited Uses**

- Meta communication or sharing out-of-character (OOC) information.
- Forcing, threatening, or pressuring another gang into a conflict or gang war through gang chat.

### **5. Situation Timing Rules**

#### **Standard Timers**

- A **Gang Meeting** must be accepted within **15 minutes** of being called.
- After a gang meeting concludes with a decision to fight, gangs are given **20 minutes** to prepare for the war.
- Once a **Gang War** is declared, the opposing gang must accept it within **10 minutes**.
- **Hostages may not be held for more than 45 minutes** under any circumstances.

#### **Pre-War Outcome**

If a gang chooses to engage in a **Pre-War Phase**, they must accept the outcome:

- **All opposing members are down** → The situation is immediately concluded. **No gang meeting or final war may follow.**
- **Not all opposing members are down** → A **mandatory gang meeting** must be held. If no compromise is reached, the situation may proceed to a **Gang War**, following the standard timers:
- 15-minute Gang Meeting response time.
- 20-minute preparation period.
- 10-minute Gang War acceptance period.
