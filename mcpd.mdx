### Respect the Police

- Treat all Police Officers with respect at all times.
- Do not be toxic, abusive, mocking, or degrading towards PD members.
- Do not tell Police Officers how to do their job or argue using server rules during an active RP.
- Do not force Police Officers into Out-of-Character (OOC) discussions.
- Police Officers have their own Roleplay to manage. Do not pressure them to process your situation faster.
- Impersonating law enforcement is strictly prohibited.<br />  ▸ This includes wearing PD uniforms, using police equipment, or operating PD vehicles.
- Do not request a higher-ranking officer to start RP.<br />  ▸ Begin with the officer on scene.<br />  ▸ Escalation will occur only if necessary.

### Roleplay Standards

- Do not declare open war against the Police Department. The Police Department is **not** a gang.
- Players may not initiate any criminal or hostile scenario during an active **Section 144**.
- Police Officers are not required to show fear in a **1 vs 1** situation.
- Crimes committed in front of PD may result in immediate arrest and processing.<br />• If a gang member commits a crime in front of PD within city limits:<br />  ▸ PD has the right to immediately attempt an arrest.<br />  ▸ Other gang members may not open fire on PD to prevent the arrest.<br />  ▸ Every gang member is responsible for avoiding criminal acts in front of PD within city limits.
- PD will cooperate in constructive, high-quality communication RP.
- If you believe you were mistreated by PD:<br />  ▸ Continue the roleplay.<br />  ▸ Do not contact staff during the active scenario.<br />  ▸ If you believe FRP occurred, open a ticket after the situation has concluded.

### Police Authority

- Police may search individuals or vehicles when they have **reasonable suspicion**.
- Carrying an illegal weapon in public may result in **search, seizure, arrest, and confiscation**.
- Police may arrest a player **anywhere**, including safe zones, gang houses, and city limits, if a valid government-issued warrant exists.
- All on-duty Police Officers may respond to unexpected emergencies, including **Section 144** situations.
- Once processed, any seized items registered as evidence cannot be returned.

### Traffic & Vehicle Rules

- Performing wheelies, burnouts, excessive horn usage, or other stunts in front of Police Officers is considered **Cop Baiting** and may result in your driving licence being revoked.
- Police may deploy **spike strips and roadblocks** before negotiations begin. These may be removed after negotiations to allow an escape Roleplay if appropriate.
- Intentionally triggering situations by pitting PD vehicles or reckless driving is prohibited and considered cop-baiting
- A pull-over announcement and emergency lights will be used to initiate a traffic stop.
- If you fail to pull over, PD may initiate a chase, request backup, and proceed with pitting if necessary.
- Baiting officers at traffic checks to lead them into another scenario is pure cop-baiting and direct FRP
- PD vehicles cannot be stolen.<br />  ▸They may only be used to escape during an active robbery if your own vehicle is                    disabled.<br />  ▸ The vehicle must be abandoned immediately after escaping.<br />  ▸ It cannot be reused to start another situation, negotiation, or chase.

### Identity & Equipment

- Wearing a **mask or helmet** during Police interactions gives Police the right to investigate, search, or take appropriate legal action.

### Combat Restrictions

- Attacking Police Officers inside Safe Zones is **Fail Roleplay (FRP)**.
- Killing a Police Officer outside of robberies or major Roleplay scenarios requires a **strong, high-quality Roleplay reason**.
- Escaping a Police chase by intentionally jumping into water to end the situation is prohibited. If you accidentally fall into the water, you must return to shore as soon as possible.
- Once eliminated, you may not rejoin the same Police situation through illegal revival or any unauthorized method.
- Using /reloadskin or /refreshskin while being tased is considered bug exploitation.
- Triggering unnecessary hostage situations involving PD is considered heavy FRP and may result in immediate removal from the server.

### Situation Restrictions

- Non-involved civilians or players may not interfere in active Police situations, robberies, shootouts, or chases.
- One-on-one threats, hostage attempts, or taser situations against Police Officers are considered **Fail Roleplay (FRP)**.
- Police Officers may **never be burned** under any circumstances.

### Police Gang Members Limits

- A maximum of **5 members** from the same gang may belong to the Police Department.
- Only **5 members** from a gang may actively serve in the **MCPD** at any given time. Any additional members must resign before another member may join.

### Duty Hours

- Police Officers should go **off duty at 1:00 AM** if there are no active situations requiring Police presence.

## FP (Fair Play Chat) Usage

- **FP (Fair Play Chat)** is reserved **exclusively for robbery-related communication**.
- Every FP message **must include**:
  - Robbery location
  - Number of robbers
  - Type of weapon(s) being used

**Example:**

> **"Shop robbery at 7015, 2 robbers with pistols."**

- FP chat **must not be MCPD** for any purpose other than robbery communication.
- The **MCPD** may respond using the weapon category specified in the FP message.
- If **no FP** is provided, the **MCPD is authorized to respond using any approved weapon** at their discretion.

# Code 99 Rules

- **Code 99** refers to any active firing situation.
- This includes robberies and any other scenario involving active gunfire.
- Only players directly involved in the scenario may remain in or enter the area.
- Any uninvolved player entering the active scene is considered to be interfering with the situation and will be considered as **FRP**.

# Code 144 Rules

- Not every situation qualifies for a **Code 144**.
  - It will only be declared when the situation becomes uncontrollable.
- Entering an active firing zone (declared **Code 144** area) after hearing gunshots is considered **No-Fear RP** and may result in punishment if done intentionally.
- If you enter an active PD firing zone, whether knowingly or unknowingly:
  - Raise your hands immediately.
  - Leave the area without interfering.
- Raising your hands indicates surrender and withdrawal from the situation.
  - Once you leave, you may not return for any reason.
- Once **Code 144** is declared:
  - All uninvolved players must immediately leave the area.
  - Remaining in the zone intentionally is considered **FRP**.
  - Engaging PD, including firing back because PD fired first, is also considered **FRP**.
- **Code 144** situations will result in respawn outcomes.

# PD vs Gang Situations

- **PD vs Gang Situations are strictly prohibited.**
